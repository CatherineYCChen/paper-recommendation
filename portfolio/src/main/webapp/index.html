About Me

Hi! My name is Catherine Yu-Chi Chen. I am currently a junior at Columbia University of New York.

Fun Fact: Columbia's school colour, Patone 290, is one of my favourite colours as well. (btw, this colour is not Pantone 290 lol)

I was born in Taipei, Taiwan in 2000. Along with my mother, and sister, I immigrated to Toronto, Canada in 2010, while my father stayed in Taiwan to work and support our family.

I studied in Canada from grade 5 to 12, and I attended high school at Earl Haig Secondary School, where I was part of the Claude Watson Arts Program as a music major specializing in the cello.

Data Science

In my senior year of high school, I took a course called Data Management, which included topics that covered probability theory, and basic regressions/analysis. For my cumulative project in that class, I conducted research on the relationship between a country's GDP per capita, and cereal consumption per capita - which I was interested in after reading the book 'Guns, Germs, and Steel'. As the book outlines, there appears to be a positive correlation between the two variables:

Upon taking this course, I became enticed by the field of data science. However, I was still new to the area, and so during my first and second year of college, I experimented with different subjects - math, chemistry, computer science, etc. However, my passion for data persisted and I ultimately declared Data Science as my major this spring. Data Science is a special joint-major that is offered by the statistics and computer science department at Columbia that was created in response to the growing significance of "big data".

Experience

Below is a list of some of my past/current working experiences:

Intern at Industrial Technology Research Institute

July 2018 - August 2018

ITRI is a leading research and development institution in Taiwan that works towards transforming Taiwan's technology industry. At ITRI, I worked in the biomaterials lab, where I assisted in conducting experiments for biomaterial development, and conducted research on the innovation of various biomaterials.

Intern at Point Robotics MedTech Inc. Intern

July 2019 - August 2019

Point Robotics is a relatively new company that develops surgical robots relying on computer vision, machine learning, and AI, to perform spinal surgeries. At Point Robotics, I developed a program for 3D image reconstruction, GUI development for the program, and implemented the program to perform 3D printing of the reconstructed models. In addition, I conducted research on various computer vision techniques in order to improve image reconstruction, as well as other recognition technologies required for the surgical robots.
